Marcus Aurelius Antoninus Augustus was a Roman emperor and stoic philosopher, known for his most famous work "Meditations".

He wrote Meditations while on campaing as a way to remind himself of the important stoic pratices to follow.

He did not write this for others to see, but as a personal journal. You can read Meditations on Wikisource.
